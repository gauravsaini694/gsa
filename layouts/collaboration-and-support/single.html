About Generation Saviour Association

GSA is currently supported by Vital Strategies and the World Health Organisation (WHO). Previously, it collaborated with the International Union Against Tuberculosis & Lung Disease (The Union) and Global Health Advocacy Incubator. GSA works closely with the Department of Health and Family Welfare in Punjab, Haryana, and Chandigarh.

The organization has trained police officials, government doctors, health professionals, and law enforcement officials under the National Tobacco Control Program (NTCP) at both state and district levels. GSA assists the Department of Health and Family Welfare in organizing State and District Level Coordination committee meetings for NTCP and participates in enforcement drives in these regions. Through one-on-one meetings, GSA facilitates inter-department coordination for effective NTCP implementation.

Generation Saviour Association conducts awareness programs on public health threats to educate current and future generations. It also organizes medical camps to support community health. GSA is known for initiatives such as Tobacco Free Buildings, Children Against Tobacco, Tobacco Free Sabzi Mandies, Healthy School Campaign, and Courtesy Notices to Tobacco Vendors.
